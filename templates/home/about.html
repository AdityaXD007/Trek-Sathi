About Nepal

Discover why Nepal is the world's premier trekking destination

Why Choose Nepal for Trekking?

Nepal is home to eight of the world's fourteen highest peaks and offers unparalleled trekking experiences

Majestic Peaks

Home to Mount Everest and seven other 8000m+ peaks, Nepal offers the world's most spectacular mountain views including Annapurna, Manaslu, and Cho Oyu.

Rich Culture

Experience authentic Sherpa, Tamang, and Gurung cultures, ancient monasteries, colorful festivals, and the warm hospitality of mountain communities.

Diverse Landscapes

From subtropical forests and terraced fields to alpine meadows, glacial valleys, and high-altitude deserts - Nepal's landscapes are incredibly diverse.

World-Class Trails

Well-established trekking routes with comfortable tea houses, experienced local guides, and excellent infrastructure for safe adventures.

Geography & Climate

Nepal is a landlocked country nestled in the heart of the Himalayas, bordered by China to the north and India to the south. Despite its small size, Nepal boasts incredible geographical diversity.

Altitude Range

From 60m in the Terai plains to 8,848m at Mount Everest's summit

Climate Zones

Tropical, subtropical, temperate, subalpine, and alpine climates

Best Seasons

Autumn (Oct-Nov) and Spring (Mar-May) offer perfect trekking conditions

Culture & Heritage

Nepal's cultural richness is as impressive as its natural beauty. The country is home to ancient traditions, vibrant festivals, and architectural marvels that tell stories of centuries past.

Hindu & Buddhist Heritage

Nepal is the birthplace of Lord Buddha and home to numerous sacred Hindu temples and Buddhist monasteries.

Mountain Communities

Experience the unique lifestyles of Sherpa, Tamang, Gurung, and Thakali people in mountain villages.

Festivals & Traditions

Participate in colorful festivals like Dashain, Tihar, and local celebrations throughout the year.
